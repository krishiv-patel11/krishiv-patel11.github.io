What is Sodium Alginate?

Sodium alginate is a natural polysaccharide extracted from brown seaweed. It is commonly used as a thickener, stabilizer, and gelling agent in various industries, including food, pharmaceuticals, and textiles.

Applications

Sodium alginate has numerous applications:

  • Food industry: Used as a thickening and gelling agent in products such as ice cream, jelly, and salad dressings.
  • Pharmaceuticals: Used as a binder in tablets and capsules.
  • Textiles: Used for textile printing and as a thickener in textile dyes.
  • Cosmetics: Used in the formulation of creams, lotions, and masks.
  • Dental impressions: Used as a material for dental molds.

Safety

Sodium alginate is generally recognized as safe (GRAS) by regulatory authorities when used in accordance with good manufacturing practices. However, individuals with a known allergy to seaweed should avoid products containing sodium alginate.

Experimental Results

The experiment investigated the effects of sodium alginate on plant growth and soil moisture retention. Here are the key findings:

  • Higher concentrations of sodium alginate hindered plant growth due to seed over-hydration.
  • Medium-low concentrations of sodium alginate (5g) showed the most optimal results, promoting enhanced growth and reducing water demand.
  • Control pots without sodium alginate exhibited moderate growth, serving as a baseline for comparison.
